Solutions

What can we Do?

Improved Training Data: Ensure that the algorithms are trained on diverse datasets that include a wide range of faces to reduce bias and improve accuracy for all racial groups.

Regular Audits: Conduct frequent independent audits of FRT systems to check for and address any biases or inaccuracies. Transparency: Require law enforcement agencies to be transparent about their use of FRT, including the sharing of data on its accuracy and impact on different racial groups.

Strict Regulations: Implement strict regulations and guidelines for the use of FRT, including clear accountability measures for misuse or errors.

Moratoriums or Bans: Consider temporary moratoriums or outright bans on the use of FRT by law enforcement until the technology is proven to be fair and reliable.

Diverse Data sets: Ensure training datasets include a wide range of demographics (age, gender, ethnicity, etc.) to reduce biases in recognition algorithms

Regulation and Legislation: Advocate for policies and regulations that govern the ethical use of facial recognition technology, ensuring accountability and preventing misuse.

Ethical Guidelines and Standards: Develop and enforce ethical guidelines and standards for the use of facial recognition technology

Public Awareness and Education: Educate the public about the potential risks of facial recognition so that one can make conscious decisions about the use of facial recognition technology.
